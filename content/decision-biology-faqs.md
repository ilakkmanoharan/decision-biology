## What is your company going to make?

Nature Foundation Models is building adaptive scientific intelligence systems for natural systems.

Our first domain is Decision Biology — AI systems that model how cells process information, adapt under uncertainty, respond to perturbations, and evolve through signaling dynamics.

We are building ASRA (Adaptive Scientific Reasoning Architecture), a reasoning engine designed for:

* world-model construction
* causal inference
* intervention learning
* hidden-mechanism discovery
* adaptive experimentation

The long-term vision is a new class of foundation models capable of reasoning across biology, chemistry, physics, and environmental systems instead of only performing static pattern prediction.

---

## What is your product and what does it do?

Decision Biology is the first scientific domain built on top of ASRA.

The system treats:

* perturbations as interventions
* signaling pathways as world models
* cellular adaptation as causal reasoning

Instead of only predicting outputs from biological datasets, the system attempts to learn causal structure, hidden dynamics, and adaptive behaviors from interaction and experimentation.

The platform is designed for:

* biological reasoning under uncertainty
* intervention analysis
* adaptive scientific discovery
* scientific hypothesis generation
* mechanism inference

Long-term, this becomes a scientific intelligence layer for natural systems.

## What is the problem you're solving?

Scientific discovery is still slow, fragmented, empirical, and highly dependent on manual experimentation.

Most AI systems today are optimized for:

* static datasets
* fixed objectives
* known action spaces
* pattern prediction

But scientific environments require:

* experimentation
* uncertainty reduction
* intervention reasoning
* hidden-mechanism discovery
* adaptive reasoning

Current AI systems are not designed for scientific intelligence.

---

## Why now?

Several things are converging:

* Foundation models demonstrated that large-scale representation learning works.
* Biology datasets are rapidly expanding (single-cell, perturbation, signaling, multimodal biology).
* Scientific AI is moving from prediction toward reasoning and experimentation.
* Advances in world models, causal inference, and agentic systems make adaptive scientific reasoning more feasible.

We believe the next major leap in AI will come from systems that can build internal models of natural systems through experimentation and interaction.

## What is new about your approach?

Most existing systems assume:

* environment semantics are already known
* action spaces are predefined
* objectives are fixed
* scientific structure is externally specified

ASRA focuses on learning hidden action semantics and causal dynamics through interaction itself.

The architecture is designed around:

* intervention-driven learning
* world-model evolution
* adaptive experimentation
* uncertainty reduction
* hidden-mechanism inference

We are interested in systems that behave less like static predictors and more like scientific investigators.
